## üîπ Security Checklist for Users

### 1. Wallet & Key Management
- Use **hardware wallets** for storing large amounts of crypto.  
- Enable **multi-signature (multi-sig) wallets** for shared control.  
- Never share private keys or seed phrases.  
- Rotate admin keys if possible and verify transactions regularly.

### 2. Platform Verification
- Confirm **smart contract addresses** before interacting.  
- Check **official communications** (website, social media, Discord).  
- Be cautious with **new or unaudited protocols**.  
- Monitor for sudden changes in **liquidity pools, bridges, or token contracts**.

### 3. Monitoring & Alerts
- Set up **on-chain alerts** (e.g., for wallet activity or large transfers).  
- Follow **trusted analytics providers** for anomaly detection.  
- Track community reports of suspicious activity.

### 4. Diversification & Risk Management
- Avoid storing all funds in one platform.  
- Consider **splitting assets across multiple protocols or wallets**.  
- Withdraw periodically to reduce exposure to potential exploits.

## üîπ What to Do if an Exploit Occurs

1. **Containment**
   - Stop interacting with affected contracts immediately.  
   - Move unaffected assets to secure wallets.  

2. **Document the Incident**
   - Record transaction hashes, wallet addresses, and timestamps.  
   - Take screenshots of dashboards or alerts.  

3. **Contact Support**
   - Notify the protocol‚Äôs official support or helpdesk.  
   - Provide detailed but **secure and non-sensitive** information.

4. **Evaluate Recovery Options**
   - Withdraw recovered funds when available.  
   - Track updates from the protocol on compensation or remediation.
